JioHotstar (formerly Disney+ Hotstar) hiked its prices in January 2026 — the Premium plan now costs Rs 2,199/year, and Hollywood content is locked behind higher tiers. If you're rethinking your streaming subscription, here are the Indian alternatives worth considering.

## ZEE5

**Best for:** Regional language content and original web series.

ZEE5 has the strongest regional content library among Indian OTT platforms. It covers Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Bengali, Marathi, Malayalam, Gujarati, and more — not just dubbed content, but originals produced in each language.

### What works well

- Largest vernacular content library in India
- Strong original web series lineup (Abhay, Sunflower, Rangbaaz)
- Premieres of Zee TV show episodes a day before TV telecast
- Free tier available with ads

### Watch out for

- Premium plan at Rs 999/year is good value, but the free tier has aggressive ads
- Sports coverage is limited compared to JioHotstar
- UI can feel cluttered with too many banners and promotions
- 4K content library is thin

### What users say

- "Great for regional content, but the app pushes too many notifications"
- "Some shows randomly disappear from the library without notice"

**Pricing:** Free with ads | Premium Rs 999/year | [Visit ZEE5](/entertainment/zee5)

## SonyLIV

**Best for:** Sports fans (especially cricket and football) and international shows.

SonyLIV has carved a niche with its sports rights — it carries UEFA Champions League, WWE, and select cricket tournaments. The originals like Scam 1992 and Rocket Boys set a high bar that newer shows try to match.

### What works well

- Sports coverage fills gaps that JioHotstar doesn't cover
- High-quality original series with genuine critical acclaim
- Clean, relatively simple UI compared to competitors
- Includes Sony TV channel content (KBC, CID, etc.)

### Watch out for

- Premium plan at Rs 999/year is reasonable, but free content is very limited
- Content library is smaller than ZEE5 or JioHotstar
- No simultaneous streaming on lower plans — only 1 device
- App can buffer on slower connections

### What users say

- "Good originals but not enough of them — you finish everything worth watching in a month"
- "The live sports experience has improved a lot, but still lags behind JioHotstar for cricket"

**Pricing:** Rs 299/month | Rs 999/year | [Visit SonyLIV](/entertainment/sonyliv)

## MX Player (Amazon MX Player)

**Best for:** Free streaming with a large library of movies and shows.

MX Player started as a video player app and evolved into a full streaming platform. Now rebranded as Amazon MX Player after Times Internet sold it to Amazon, it offers a massive free content library supported by ads.

### What works well

- Huge free library — movies, web series, and TV shows at no cost
- Good selection of international dramas (Korean, Turkish, Thai)
- Offline download available even on the free tier
- Started as a video player — still the best local video player on Android

### Watch out for

- Amazon acquisition means it's transitioning — some content overlap with Prime Video
- Ads on the free tier can be intrusive (unskippable 30-second ads)
- Original content quality is inconsistent
- The "MX Player" local player and "MX Player" streaming are still confusingly bundled

### What users say

- "Best free option in India but the ads are getting worse"
- "Since Amazon took over, some content has moved to Prime Video behind the paywall"

**Pricing:** Free with ads | [Visit MX Player](/entertainment/mx-player)

## Bottom line

There's no single Indian platform that replaces everything JioHotstar offers — especially if you watch IPL cricket. But most people don't use every feature of their streaming service.

If you primarily watch **regional content**, ZEE5 is the better choice. If you care about **quality originals and sports beyond cricket**, SonyLIV delivers. If you just want **free movies and shows**, MX Player has the largest no-cost library in India.

The practical move for many households: pick one paid service (ZEE5 or SonyLIV at Rs 999/year) and supplement with MX Player's free tier. That's Rs 999 versus JioHotstar's Rs 2,199 Premium plan, and you'll likely watch more content you actually enjoy.
